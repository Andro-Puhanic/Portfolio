Logo design and brand guidelines

Lyra Systems was one of those projects that make you stop for a moment, take a deeper breath, and notice how powerful quiet technology can be. Their world is built around the belief that predicting is not a luxury, but a responsibility. A responsibility toward the systems that power cities, industries, and everyday life. In this task, my goal was to turn their Brand Core Summary into a clear, coherent, and visually strong story. The entire process was conceptual; every phase, from analysis to execution, was done independently, without outside input, to test my own limits and creative power.

My Personal Perspective

Working on Lyra’s identity felt like shaping maps of the future. Quiet, careful, and demanding, but deeply rewarding. From the start, I knew this project would not accept shortcuts. Even though this was a conceptual project, I approached it as if it were real. As a designer, I’m fascinated when a brand has the courage to stay humble, to build identity not through shouting, but through integrity. Lyra Systems is exactly that: a calm, precise system that creates impact through intelligence, not aggression. It is a brand that lives between data and sustainability, on that thin line between human responsibility and technological elegance.

A bit of astronomy first

Lyra, constellation in the northern sky at about 18 hours right ascension and 40° north in declination. Its brightest star is Vega, the fifth brightest star in the sky, with a magnitude of 0.03. With the bright stars Deneb and Altair, Vega is part of the prominent asterism of the Summer Triangle.

The company I created this logo and visual identity for is called LYRA. Since they work in AI, my first instinct was to design something connected to technology. But after taking a closer look at the brief, I realized they are more than just a tech company. They are fighting to protect our planet. By combining huge amounts of data with AI analysis, they manage to reduce the energy costs of cities on a massive scale.

That’s why I decided to return into nature.
