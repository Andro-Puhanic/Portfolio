My Work CONTACT

Logo and brand design

My task in this project was to translate their Brand Core Summary into a clear and emotional visual direction. The whole process was conceptual, done independently as a way to explore how far I can go in shaping a brand. Shadow is not meant to be loud or trendy. It is meant to feel like a familiar breath, a soft pause in the middle of a busy day. Working on this project meant capturing the soul of a place that doesn’t try to impress you. It welcomes you, without forcing anything. The challenge was to create an identity that is intimate, calm, and honest, while still feeling modern and intentional.

This was AWESOME!

Even though the project was conceptual, I am really proud of the result. Shadow feels like a brand that doesn’t need to convince anyone of its value; it simply exists, and people find meaning in the peace it offers. Designing the identity for Shadow Café was a very emotional and intuitive experience. Unlike tech-based projects, this one lived in senses: warm light, wooden textures, gentle sounds, the quiet moment when someone takes the first sip of coffee. Being a huge coffee fan myself, presenting the feeling of that beautiful warm liquid through design was an incredibly personal task.

From the shadows...

The first step was studying the Brand Core Summary and identifying the key emotions: warmth, calmness, and belonging. I turned the emotional insights into concrete design guidelines: colors inspired by wood and soft light, round shapes that feel gentle, textures that suggest calm spaces and slow mornings. I tried many versions of typography, symbol ideas, and layout rhythms, always searching for the one that feels warm and timeless.

And the result did not disappoint...
