Logo design and brand guidelines

Calcalc is an application designed to track consumed and input calories throughout the day and across individual meals. What sets it apart is its focus on traditional Balkan cuisine. Many people across the region struggle with the lack of reliable nutritional information, as dishes like gulaš, paprikaš, sarma, or čorba are neither widely recognized nor represented in the mainstream fitness world. Calcalc aims to change that. By bringing structure, clarity, and relevance to local eating habits, the app gives users the ability to understand their diet through tools tailored specifically to Balkan food culture. Its primary audience includes young adults and middle-aged individuals who lack the time to manually research or calculate how many more krofne they can enjoy without guilt.

My Personal Perspective

I was fortunate to be entrusted with the task of translating Calcalc’s vision into a cohesive visual identity. With my background and experience in the fitness field, this project became the perfect opportunity to showcase how design can empower a community that often feels overlooked due to insufficient nutritional guidance. Creating this identity meant shaping a brand for people who seek clarity, confidence, and a sense of representation in their everyday diet. After a detailed analysis of the company’s goals and aspirations, I gained a clear understanding of the direction the brand needed to take—and how I could bring that vision to life for these Balkan innovators.

What's my proces...?

Communicating

Creating the conditions needed to uncover every detail required to translate their vision into something tangible. This stage laid the foundation for understanding not just what they wanted, but why those elements mattered to the project.

Understanding

Reviewing the meetings and questionnaires completed by the project leads. With carefully structured questions, I was able to extract the insights necessary to shape a solution that aligns with their expectations.

Creating

A long cycle of refinement, experimentation, and open communication between myself and the creative team at Calcalc. I’m convinced that clear dialogue and honesty are essential for achieving the best possible outcome.
